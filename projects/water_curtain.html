Lift+Cruise eVTOL Aerodynamic Design & Optimization

Conceptual design and multidisciplinary aerodynamic optimization of a 4-passenger electric vertical take-off and landing (eVTOL) aircraft focused on efficiency, low noise, and urban integration. Performed as part of sustainable aviation initiatives and SAE Aero Design leadership experience.

Led a student team to 1st place (Regional) & Top 10 (International) in SAE Aero Design 20XX.

My Role

  • Team Captain & Lead Aerodynamics
  • Conceptual & detailed design
  • Low & high-fidelity CFD (XFOIL, SU2, OpenFOAM)
  • Propeller-wing interaction analysis
  • Wind-tunnel testing coordination
  • Technical documentation & presentation

Tools & Skills

  • OpenVSP, SolidWorks, CATIA
  • XFOIL, AVL, OpenFOAM
  • Python automation & optimization
  • Low-speed wind tunnel testing

Presentation Design Report GitHub Repo

Design Objectives

Targeted urban air mobility with vertical takeoff, efficient cruise, and minimal acoustic footprint. Key requirements included hover efficiency > 12 kg/kW, cruise L/D > 12 in cruise, and compliance with emerging EASA/FAA certification guidelines for distributed electric propulsion (DEP).

Results & Achievements

Achieved 1st place in SAE Aero Design Regular Class (Asia-Pacific region) and 9th internationally. The design demonstrated excellent stability, payload capacity, and aerodynamic efficiency validated through CFD and wind-tunnel testing.
